The Apicoplast as a Drug Target

Quick Facts

  • The Apicomplexa comprise a phylum of mostly intracellular parasitic protists that mainly infect mammals, including humans
    • Plasmodium spp. (malaria)
    • Toxoplasma gondii (toxoplasmosis)
    • Babesia spp. (babesiosis)
    • etc.
  • There is a difficulty in treating these infections because of the fact that they are eukaryotes as well.
  • However, with the exception of one genus, all possess a endosymbiotic plastid termed the apicoplast
    • This has a cyanobacterial ancestry that can be used as a drug target, as without it the cells die
    • For this reason, antibiotics like fosmidomycin and doxycycline are able to target the apicoplast
  • The function of the apicoplast is not fully understood, but certain pathways have been identified
    • Isoprenoid precursor synthesis
      • Fosmidomycin targets this
    • Fatty acid synthesis
    • Partial haem synthesis
